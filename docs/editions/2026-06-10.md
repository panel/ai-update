# Beeg Model Smell

*Anthropic dropped Claude Fable 5 and the people who build coding agents for a living are uninstalling their IDEs again — meanwhile the labs are openly fighting about whether the frontier should be allowed to improve itself.*

## The Big Picture

The period belongs to one release. **Claude Fable 5** landed in Claude Code and Cowork, and the reaction from people who have lived through every Anthropic launch is unusually consistent: this is a major-version-bump step change, [the first of its kind since Opus 4.5 in November](https://nitter.net/alexalbert__/status/2064394410004304003#m). Karpathy frames it as [SOTA "by a margin" and qualitatively a different thing](https://nitter.net/karpathy/status/2064409694761054332#m) — the model "gets" ambitious tasks you wouldn't previously have handed off. swyx, rerunning his own benchmarks, says the official charts [undersell the takeoff: it "breaks every curve fit"](https://nitter.net/swyx/status/2064414823748886591#m) because it's a different class of model. Take the breathless launch-week tone with appropriate salt, but the signal across independent observers is strong enough to treat as real.

What actually changed, if you read past the superlatives, is *autonomy*. The recurring phrase is the shift from directing a tool to collaborating with a partner — Boris Cherny [uninstalled his IDE](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064431111154053187#m) and now describes the model as a "thought and design partner" with "judgement, taste, and dimensionality." The practical implication is that your scaffolding from the last model generation is now actively holding you back. Alex Albert's [usage tips](https://nitter.net/alexalbert__/status/2064467657483829441#m) are blunt: give it bigger tasks, default to high effort, and *rewrite your skills and CLAUDE.md files* because instructions tuned for prior models "anchor Fable to stale patterns."

The counter-melody is a sharpening fight about safety and power. Dario Amodei's [Policy on the AI Exponential](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48480719) drew a pointed rebuttal from Jeremy Howard, who argues Anthropic has [chosen "the opposite of the safe path"](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064595820952064054#m) — reserving its top model for its own frontier research while signaling it would slow others. And François Chollet offered a useful deflationary frame for the whole boom: [it can be a bubble even if the tech works](https://nitter.net/fchollet/status/2064740102463725853#m). Worth holding both thoughts at once.

## Themes

### Fable 5 and the "objectives, not tasks" workflow

The model is the headline, but the durable takeaway is a workflow shift. The thing every Anthropic engineer keeps circling is **self-verification**: in the age of long-running agents, building loops that let the model check its own work is [the key ingredient that lets it run longer and land closer to intent](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064426115255730578#m) without constant babysitting. Fable is described as better at exactly this — [more efficient tokens, better tool use, more intelligent self-verification, higher autonomy](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064402671898075579#m). The user-facing corollary, per Albert, is to [move from providing tasks to providing objectives](https://nitter.net/alexalbert__/status/2064467657483829441#m) and let the model exercise its own judgment first.

There's also a free-lunch window worth exploiting: swyx notes [a lot of "alpha" in pointing Claude Code at "review my code for issues" on Fable while it's not yet pay-per-use](https://nitter.net/swyx/status/2064492823781789969#m) — and warns you'll be horrified by what you already shipped.

Go deeper: [Cherny on Fable as design partner](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064431111154053187#m), [Albert's four tips](https://nitter.net/alexalbert__/status/2064467657483829441#m), [self-verification loops](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064426115255730578#m), [Karpathy's qualitative read](https://nitter.net/karpathy/status/2064409694761054332#m).

### Agents spawning agents

The structural news inside Claude Code: **nested subagent support** has [landed, capped at depth=5](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064327225504403752#m), with agents kicking off agents as a context-management strategy. This is the same idea OpenAI's side is pushing from a different angle — gdb's framing of Codex as ["an AI teammate instead of just an AI assistant"](https://nitter.net/gdb/status/2063705280270021087#m), and his sharper observation that when he *doesn't* reach for Codex it's [usually missing context or a missing skill, rarely a capability ceiling — "the overhang feels large"](https://nitter.net/gdb/status/2063437915347136554#m). Early signal, but the mental model converging across both labs is: the bottleneck is now your orchestration and context engineering, not the model.

Go deeper: [nested subagents](https://nitter.net/bcherny/status/2064327225504403752#m), [Codex as teammate](https://nitter.net/gdb/status/2063705280270021087#m), [the overhang](https://nitter.net/gdb/status/2063437915347136554#m).

### The safety fight gets specific

The abstract "should we slow down?" debate got concrete this week. Dario Amodei's [Policy on the AI Exponential](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48480719) (102 points, 152 comments) is the anchor; Jeremy Howard's response is the part worth reading for tension. His argument: if you genuinely believe in slowing recursive self-improvement, [you should ensure *your own org* can't use its best model for frontier research](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064597094741192874#m) — otherwise you're just [advancing the frontier and widening the power imbalance](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064595820952064054#m) under a safety banner. The "Sophanthropic" jab about [sabotaging others' experiments to protect a lead](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064613063370977559#m) is rhetoric, but the underlying critique — that "safe path" framing can mask incumbent advantage — is the sharpest disagreement in the field right now.

Go deeper: [Amodei's post + HN thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48480719), [Howard's "open it up" position](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064597094741192874#m), [the power-imbalance critique](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064595820952064054#m).

### Bubble logic, stated cleanly

Cut through the optimism and the doom with Chollet's framing, which is the cleanest piece of reasoning this period: a thing can be [a bubble even when the tech works, even with product-market fit, even with a path to viability](https://nitter.net/fchollet/status/2064740102463725853#m) — if profitability takes too long or kills margins. Independently, swyx's note that it was [34 days from signing an NVIDIA deal to GA of a Mythos-class model](https://nitter.net/swyx/status/2064421542503797186#m) is the bull case for velocity. Hold them together: capability and timelines are not the same axis as economics.

Go deeper: [Chollet on bubbles](https://nitter.net/fchollet/status/2064740102463725853#m), [swyx on 34-day shipping](https://nitter.net/swyx/status/2064421542503797186#m), [Chollet on knowledge vs. intelligence](https://nitter.net/fchollet/status/2063350697626845639#m).

## Don't Miss

- **The CLAUDE.md migration is real work, not optional.** If you use Claude Code, treat the Fable launch as a prompt to audit your skills and config files — [instructions written for older models now degrade performance](https://nitter.net/alexalbert__/status/2064467657483829441#m). This is the kind of invisible debt that quietly caps what a better model can do for you.
- **Read Howard's thread alongside Amodei's post, not after it.** The [open-it-up-and-democratize argument](https://nitter.net/jeremyphoward/status/2064597094741192874#m) is the strongest counter to incumbent-safety framing currently circulating, and the disagreement maps directly onto which labs you choose to build on.
